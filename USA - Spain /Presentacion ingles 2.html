Phase 2 · How to Buy
The 8-Step Buying Process
1
Get your NIE — Número de Identidad de Extranjero. Your Spanish tax ID. Required for everything. 1–2 weeks. We handle it.
2
Open a Spanish bank account — Required for the purchase transaction. We connect you to the right international banks.
3
Define your strategy — Investment, holiday home, or residency? Your goal determines your ideal property type and location.
4
Property search & selection — We pre-select properties matching your exact profile. Review digitally or during a VIP Discovery Trip.
5
Legal due diligence — Title check, debts, planning permissions, charges. NEVER skip this. Fuster & Associates handles it entirely.
6
Reservation agreement + deposit — Typically €3,000–€10,000 to secure the property while due diligence completes.
7
Private purchase contract — Full terms agreed and signed. Typically 10% deposit paid at this stage.
8
Completion at notary — Sign the title deed. Keys in hand. Welcome to Spain. Can be done remotely via Power of Attorney.

Legal & Tax · Fuster & Associates
What Every American Buyer Must Know
US / Spain Double Taxation Treaty
You will NOT pay tax twice. The treaty between the US and Spain governs how your income and assets are treated in both countries. We structure your purchase correctly from day one.
Non-Resident Income Tax
If you rent your property: you pay Spanish tax on rental income. Rate: 19% for non-residents (treaty-protected). Fully manageable with correct quarterly filings.
Capital Gains
When you sell: CGT applies in Spain (typically 19% for non-residents). Proper planning significantly reduces the exposure. We advise before, not after.
Inheritance & Succession
Critical for non-residents. Without the correct structure, Spanish inheritance law applies. Spanish will + correct ownership structure protects your family from day 1.
Power of Attorney
You can complete the entire purchase — from offer to keys — without flying to Spain. We act on your behalf with a duly notarized POA. Simple, safe, and legal.
